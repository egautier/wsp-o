Project Analysis

Client:Florida Department Highway Safety and Motor Vehicles

Project:Web Re-design

Designer:Elias Gautier

Site Intent

The Composition of the page looks too tight for the view. Website needs more space for the eyes to breathe. Using the Markup Validation Service W3C, it shows 31 errors and 7 warnings which by applying a new design will work on those. Visual design is boring and confusing, too many things together in such a small view. Needs to be fixed.

For better website's user interface/experience my redesign will consist in the following changes:

  1. Organize the entire website into 6 pages.
  2. Build a header with logo, nav bar , footer for all pages.
  3. Build homepage with small summary of the site along with special promotions the site offers.
  4. Placing a fast entrance to most visited parts of the site in the home page.(ex. Driver's license check)
  5. Make Florida residents and visitors feel more comfortable with the site also allowing them to do more through the site in their homes.
  6. Create a more percents of refers by applying more key words and also more time in the site having visitors looking at more than one page.

Competition

As competition in the state of Florida for this type of service, the site has 2 competitors; the DMV.org which is a private organization and dmvflorida.org whichs belongs to the goverment as well.

Research shows that the dmv.org private site works for the hole country with a rate ranking of 1,519 in the US and the dmvflorida.org with the higher rate of all of them with 15,341 in the US which tells me the site needs a better set of keywords and more popularity.

Targeted Audience

As the Alexa.com sites shows for the site it tells us that the site has an audience that tends to be caucasican, "disproportionally childless women between the ages of 35 and 65 with no postgrade education and an income of over $30,000.00."
